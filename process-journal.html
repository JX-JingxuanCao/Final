Week 1

Week 1, started with a decent introduction lecture. The lecture gave me a general idea on what is a good and a bad user interface. From the overview, I am very excited to see there are so many people who worked for big companies are coming to give the lecture. From the slides in the lecture, I can definitely see this course is well organised by Hamish. The rest of the lecture was given by Oliver Weidlich, an experienced mobile user experience designer. His lecture was about user centred design and how to design a great user experience.

Previous Next

For the tutorial, we did three exercises on designing. Each exercise was given multiple tasks to do and every time we finished, we had a sharing time and a reflection. The door, the microwave and the microwave for the kids. Clearly, these exercises were great and they represented each step that all designers should do. The tutor mentioned the importance of the design process cycle and I can see how I should follow those steps and use them to design in the future.

Week 2

Our tutorial was focused on different stages of the design process. I think from this tutorial the biggest take away is “designing the right things” and “designing things right”. One important that they taught today is to break down the design process and discover the stakeholder of your problem area. So the four of us started to think about which problem area we should focus on.

During the studio, the four of us did one affinity diagram on what is the current problem that you want to solve. Then when we look at the diagram, we grouped each common problem to one group and think of if these problems can be solved by automated devices or drones. I found affinity diagram is such a useful way for designers to find more and to think more on the subjects. We didn’t decide what problem area we should look at.

Research Journal

Day 1: My area is family and homes. The first thing came to my mind is Central Park. I heard rumors about that apartment building has issues with watering and insect’s problem due to the windows are so close to the vertical garden. I immediately had a few survey questions in my mind that can help me to really understand the living experiences. Residents living experiences can depend on the time they lived there, therefore, there was a question about how long they live there, it can help me to see if they had different experiences due to the time they lived there. As well as a straightforward question like do you have ever experience watering and maintenance issues due to living so close to the plants and flowers. I sent the survey to my friends, sadly there were only six of my friends who rich enough to live in Central Park. After sending the survey I started to contact those who have a backyard garden in their houses and I planned when should I go interviewing them about their farming experiences.

Day 2: All six surveys were filled and when I looked at the results, it was totally different from what I was expecting. I decided to find the apartment building manager to ask them a few questions regarding how they are watering and maintaining the vertical garden without making problems to the residents. I went to Central Park sales team, hopefully, they can give me some answers because they are selling the apartment. But unfortunately, they told me to go to central park level 2 to see the central manager and they do not work on weekends. So I have to wait until Monday to visit them again. My next plan was to interview 2 people and I met them in Townhall, the interviews were causal and small but I learned a lot from them.

Day 3: A very disappointing day of my research, I went to see the central manager they gave me a number which I called happens to be the security number, on the phone they told me to go ask the sales team because they know all the things about the apartment. I went to the same place again and they told me the watering and maintenance are all handled by Sydney council. Looks like a dead end so I moved on to my next target which is USYD food co-op. I went to the store and told my purpose of visit and they sent an email about my request of interviewing those who in charge of the garden but it will take one or two days. So I went to see one of a friend who lived in Central Park and did an interview. I wanted to learn more about their experiences in central park and I did.

Day 4: So far I did 3 interviews about backyard farming because I only know 3 people who lived in Sydney that has a backyard farm. And one interview about living in Central Park. I started to analyse my research and making it into a report and I can see a common problem that working adults faced is lack of time. Since USYD food co-op did not reply me and I went online to find some decentralized farmers. I found a forum about farming in Australia, I mainly looked at those who have problems in their farms. I found some farmers lacking experiences which results in their plants and vegetables were overwatering or too much chemical in the soil.

Day 5: I received an email from USYD food co-op, another disappointing news, they do not have a farm. So my research plan failed and I kept digging more farmers online. I did one online interview about backyard farming, but the farmer is from China. I used this interview to compare the rest of the interviews that I have done. They are quite similar.

Day 6: making the final report.
